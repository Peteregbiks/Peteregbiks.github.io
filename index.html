Robot-aided bullet retrieval

Role: Robotics Programmer

Project type: School

Duration: March 2024 to April 2024

Description

Programmed a Meca 500 robot arm to execute a specialized teleoperation task aimed at assisting in bullet removal. The system utilized an electromagnet at the end effector which is activated upon reaching the bullet. The arm was programmed to precisely maneuver to a defined incision point. Once the robot arm reached this point, the speed was significantly reduced, and the arm transitioned from 3 degrees of freedom (DOF) to 6 DOF for enhanced control and accuracy. This project provided valuable insights into robotic programming, precision control, and the importance of safety in medical applications.

Role

  • Developed and implemented the control algorithms for the Meca 500 robot arm to perform teleoperated task with the aid of a haptic device.
  • Programmed the transition of the robot arm from 3 DOF to 6 DOF upon reaching the defined incision point to enhance operational precision.
  • Integrated an electromagnet into the robotic arm's end effector for effective bullet retrieval.

Key Challenge

I encounted issues with maintaining precision during the bullet removal operation as it is critical due to the sensitivity of the procedure.

Solution

I established a reduced workspace for the robot arm in relation to the haptic device's workspace upon reaching the incision point. This adjustment ensured high precision in the arm's movements by implementing a speed reduction protocol, effectively minimizing the risk of error.

Tools and Softwares Used

Meca500 Robot arm, Haply Inverse3 Haptic device, electromagnet, and Matlab.
